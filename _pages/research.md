## 2.1 Academic Research Projects

### Elephant Detection and Early Warning Using Seismic Waves

This Master of Philosophy research focused on developing a non-invasive technological approach for mitigating human–elephant conflict in Sri Lanka. The study investigated the long-range detection of elephants through seismic signals generated by elephant locomotion, which were captured, processed, and classified using a custom embedded sensing system and machine learning models.

The study was guided by two primary objectives: (1) to design and develop a passive detection system that captures the seismic signals of elephants, and (2) to develop a detection model/system which identifies seismic signatures from Sri Lankan elephants. In response to these objectives, the research introduced an instrumentation system comprising a novel geophone–sensor interface for long-range seismic signal acquisition, a novel signal processing method for event detection termed the Contextually Customized Window, and a machine learning-based framework for classifying seismic signals and identifying elephant-generated seismic events under both controlled and natural environmental conditions. The work was further extended through the application of explainable artificial intelligence techniques to interpret model decisions and identify the most influential characteristics of elephant footfall seismic signals.

The study demonstrated strong performance outcomes as follows:
* Detect an elephant locomotion at a maximum distance of 155.6 meters

The classification model performed:
* 99% accuracy for controlled settings (Tested up to 155.6 m)
* 73% accuracy for natural environment (Tested up to 140 m)
* 70% accuracy for HEC-prone village areas

To the best of the authors’ knowledge at the conclusion of the MPhil study in June 2023, the reported detection range represented the maximum documented range in comparable studies involving both Asian and African elephants, while the classification performance achieved in natural environments was among the highest reported under similar conditions.

This research resulted in multiple publications in a high-impact Q1 journal ranked within the top 10% in multidisciplinary research as listed:
* J. L. Wijayaraja, J. L. Wijekoon, M. Wijesundara and L. J. Mendis Wickramasinghe, "Toward Long Range Detection of Elephants Using Seismic Signals: A Geophone-Sensor Interface for Embedded Systems," in IEEE Access, vol. 12, pp. 72210-72223, 2024.
* J. L. Wijayaraja, J. L. Wijekoon and M. Wijesundara, "Event Detection and Classification for Long Range Sensing of Elephants Using Seismic Signals," in IEEE Access, vol. 13, pp. 158366-158384, 2025.

The significance of the research was further recognized by both governmental and non-governmental sectors in Sri Lanka, resulting in the following awards:
* ICT Student Award – Postgraduate Category, Computer Society of Sri Lanka, 2023 
* Bronze Award (Postgraduate Category), National ICT Awards – NBQSA Sri Lanka, 2023

The research is currently being extended in several directions, including the pruning and deployment of the proposed classification framework in resource-constrained environments and the introduction of dynamic range-based approaches for seismic signal detection.

Importantly, the knowledge, technical expertise, and motivation developed through this research contributed to the establishment of Farm’s Tech (Pvt) Ltd, with the aim of advancing innovative technological solutions for agricultural challenges and supporting their practical translation into real-world applications.

**Research Areas:** Embedded systems engineering, seismic signal processing, signal filtering, feature extraction, machine learning, explainable AI, electronic system design, sensor instrumentation, and wildlife monitoring technology.  
**Role:** MPhil Researcher (Concept development, research design, field data collection, instrumentation development, signal processing, machine learning model development, explainable AI analysis, experimental evaluation, and scholarly publications)

### Evaluation of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Based System for Automated Viva Assessments in STEM Education

This ongoing research investigates the use of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)-based framework as an automated and scalable alternative to conventional viva voce assessments in STEM education. The study develops a structured autonomous viva assessment system that integrates vector-based retrieval, prompt engineering, few-shot calibration, and LLM-based reasoning to generate personalized viva questions, evaluate student responses, and support transparent, context-aware assessment. The proposed framework was evaluated in a real-world viva setting and demonstrated strong performance in question relevance, diversity, grading alignment, and consistency relative to manual assessment. The system was evaluated at the Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology across multiple subject domains, involving more than 250 students. Beyond its practical applicability, the study has also resulted in the following ongoing publication:

*A Hybrid LLM-Optimized Framework for Autonomous Viva Assessment Using Retrieval Augmented Generation and Prompt Engineering (IEEE Access, under review)*

**Research Areas:** Retrieval-augmented generation, large language models, automated assessment, prompt engineering, educational AI, natural language processing, human–AI evaluation alignment, and higher education technology.  
**Role:** Principal Investigator  
**Contributions:** Concept development, research design, system architecture, prototype development, RAG pipeline design, prompt engineering, experimental analysis, and scholarly publication.

### Machine Learning for Flood Forecasting and Analysis in Sri Lanka

This study investigated the application of machine learning to flood forecasting and analysis in Sri Lanka, with particular focus on water level prediction and the influence of catchment areas on flood behaviour. Conducted in collaboration with the Disaster Management Division, Ministry of Defence, Sri Lanka, the research introduced an LSTM-based prediction framework integrated with explainable artificial intelligence to improve both predictive accuracy and interpretability. A context-based preprocessing method was developed to impute missing rainfall observations according to the seasonal characteristics of the study area, thereby improving the quality of the input data used for modelling. The framework achieved low prediction error, while explainable AI analysis identified the most influential catchment contributions to flood behaviour in the target region of the Kalu Ganga River basin. The study demonstrated the value of interpretable machine learning approaches for strengthening flood forecasting and disaster preparedness in Sri Lanka. 

**Publication:** J. L. Wijayaraja, N. D. U. Gamage, P. V. N. U. Pallegangoda, N. Priyashantha, W. B. M. R. Weerasekara and P. K. W. Abeygunawardhana, "Understanding Catchment Impacts: Enhanced Water Level Prediction with LSTM and Explainable AI," 2024 6th International Conference on Advancements in Computing (ICAC), Colombo.

This work was further recognized through the following award:
* **Outstanding Paper Award**, International Conference on Advancements in Computing, 2024  

**Research Areas:** LSTM modelling, explainable AI, disaster informatics, environmental data preprocessing, Flood forecasting and hydrological time-series analysis  
**Role:** Principal Investigator

### Improving the Accuracy of Automobile RPM Sensing Using Ignition Pulses from Distributor-Based Ignition Systems

This study investigated signal processing techniques to improve the accuracy of automobile RPM sensing using ignition pulses from distributor-based ignition systems. By utilizing the primary ignition pulse signal, the research proposed a less mechanically intrusive alternative to conventional sensing methods and evaluated multiple filter designs to improve measurement reliability. The findings showed that a simple RC filter could effectively mitigate noise and transients while enabling accurate and stable RPM detection, highlighting the potential of the method for embedded automotive systems and vehicle retrofitting applications.

**Publication:** J. L. Wijayaraja, N. Abhayasinghe and S. Wellalage, "Towards Accurate RPM Detection Using Primary Ignition Pulses," IECON 2023- 49th Annual Conference of the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society, Singapore.

* **IES Student and Young Professionals Paper Assistance**, 49th Annual Conference of the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society.

**Research Areas:** Signal processing, automotive electronics, embedded systems, electronic instrumentation, filter design  
**Role:** Principal Investigator

### AI-Based Diagnostic Model for Forensic Wound Interpretation: Differentiating Lacerations from Sharp - Force Injuries

This ongoing study investigates the application of computer vision to forensic wound interpretation, with particular focus on enhancing forensic image quality and developing a diagnostic model to differentiate lacerations from sharp-force injuries. The research further examines the influence of image features on the classification process in order to improve the interpretability and justification of model decisions. This study is being conducted in collaboration with Faculty of Medicine, University of Colombo.

**Research Areas:** Image processing, forensic medicine, computer vision, artificial intelligence, explainable AI  
**Role:** Co-Investigators

### Computer Vision-Based Motion Analysis and Motion Amplification for Defect Identification in Mechanical Systems, Particularly Vehicular Systems

This self-initiated project investigates the application of computer vision techniques to analyze subtle motion in mechanical systems for defect identification and condition assessment, with particular relevance to vehicular systems. Motion amplification is used to enhance imperceptible vibrations, enabling clearer observation of dynamic mechanical behaviour and supporting the identification of defects through non-contact visual analysis.

### Encrypted Radio for Defence Forces

This undergraduate research project focused on the design and development of a secure analogue radio communication platform for defence applications in Sri Lanka. Conducted in collaboration with the Centre for Defence Research and Development, Ministry of Defence, Sri Lanka, the study addressed the need for a low-cost, field-operable, and secure tactical communication system suitable for local operational requirements. The project introduced an analogue FM radio system operating in VHF and UHF bands and incorporated multiple security-oriented mechanisms, including audio encryption and frequency hopping spread spectrum, to strengthen confidentiality, integrity, and availability in tactical communication. The study further investigated transceiver selection, embedded interface design, synchronisation mechanisms, and hardware implementation, resulting in a functional prototype with strong potential for further development toward a deployable defence communication platform.

**Research Areas:** Embedded systems, RF communication, defence electronics, secure communication systems, frequency hopping, electronic system design  
**Role:** Undergraduate Researcher

### Animal repellent system design deployment and Impact Analysis

This translational project was carried out in the capacity of Co-Founder and R&D Engineer at Farm’s Tech (Pvt) Ltd and focused on the design, development, and field deployment of animal repellent systems for agricultural settings. The systems combined acoustic and light-based deterrence mechanisms to mitigate wildlife intrusion in farmlands. The work included device engineering, field evaluation of system effectiveness, and the development of low-cost manufacturing strategies to enable scalable production. More than 400 repellent devices have since been deployed across Sri Lanka, and the technology has been recognized by relevant government organizations as a sustainable solution for animal intrusion management. A field survey representing 75 users and approximately 100 deployed devices recorded a 98% satisfaction rate, indicating strong user acceptance and practical effectiveness in real-world agricultural contexts.

### Peacock Repellent Device: Additive Manufacturing-Friendly Mechanical Design with Flexible Structures

This ongoing project focuses on the mechanical design of an attachment for an animal repellent system developed by Farm’s Tech (Pvt) Ltd, with particular emphasis on peacock repellent applications addressing a significant agricultural challenge in Sri Lanka. The study explores the development of an additive manufacturing-friendly flexible structure capable of sustaining the vibration characteristics required for effective repellence, while maintaining structural integrity over prolonged use without significant deformation. Analytical evaluations are being conducted to optimize vibration performance and support the mechanical design process. Contributions to the project include concept formulation and partial involvement in 3D modelling.
