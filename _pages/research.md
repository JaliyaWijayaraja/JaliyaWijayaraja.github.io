### 2.1.1 Elephant Detection and Early Warning Using Seismic Waves <span class="status-badge status-completed">Completed</span> {#elephant-detection}

This Master of Philosophy research focused on developing a non-invasive technological approach for mitigating human–elephant conflict in Sri Lanka. The study investigated the long-range detection of elephants through seismic signals generated by elephant locomotion, which were captured, processed, and classified using a custom embedded sensing system and machine learning models.

The study was guided by two primary objectives: (1) to design and develop a passive detection system that captures the seismic signals of elephants, and (2) to develop a detection model/system which identifies seismic signatures from Sri Lankan elephants. In response to these objectives, the research introduced an instrumentation system comprising a novel geophone–sensor interface for long-range seismic signal acquisition, a novel signal processing method for event detection termed the Contextually Customized Window, and a machine learning-based framework for classifying seismic signals and identifying elephant-generated seismic events under both controlled and natural environmental conditions. The work was further extended through the application of explainable artificial intelligence techniques to interpret model decisions and identify the most influential characteristics of elephant footfall seismic signals.

The study demonstrated strong performance outcomes:

- Detected elephant locomotion at a maximum distance of **155.6 meters**
- Classification model performance:
  - **99%** accuracy for controlled settings (tested up to 155.6 m)
  - **73%** accuracy for natural environments (tested up to 140 m)
  - **70%** accuracy for HEC-prone village areas

To the best of the authors' knowledge at the conclusion of the MPhil study in June 2023, the reported detection range represented the maximum documented range in comparable studies involving both Asian and African elephants, while the classification performance achieved in natural environments was among the highest reported under similar conditions.

### 2.1.2 Evaluation of a RAG-Based System for Automated Viva Assessments in STEM Education <span class="status-badge status-under-review">Under Review</span> {#rag-viva-assessment}

This ongoing research investigates the use of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)-based framework as an automated and scalable alternative to conventional viva voce assessments in STEM education. The study develops a structured autonomous viva assessment system that integrates vector-based retrieval, prompt engineering, few-shot calibration, and LLM-based reasoning to generate personalized viva questions, evaluate student responses, and support transparent, context-aware assessment. The proposed framework was evaluated in a real-world viva setting and demonstrated strong performance in question relevance, diversity, grading alignment, and consistency relative to manual assessment. The system was evaluated at the Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology across multiple subject domains, involving more than **250 students**. Beyond its practical applicability, the study has also resulted in the following ongoing publication:

- A Hybrid LLM-Optimized Framework for Autonomous Viva Assessment Using Retrieval Augmented Generation and Prompt Engineering (*IEEE Access*, under review)

### 2.1.3 Machine Learning for Flood Forecasting and Analysis in Sri Lanka <span class="status-badge status-completed">Completed</span> {#flood-forecasting}

This study investigated the application of machine learning to flood forecasting and analysis in Sri Lanka, with particular focus on water level prediction and the influence of catchment areas on flood behaviour. Conducted in collaboration with the Disaster Management Division, Ministry of Defence, Sri Lanka, the research introduced an LSTM-based prediction framework integrated with explainable artificial intelligence to improve both predictive accuracy and interpretability. A context-based preprocessing method was developed to impute missing rainfall observations according to the seasonal characteristics of the study area, thereby improving the quality of the input data used for modelling. The framework achieved low prediction error, while explainable AI analysis identified the most influential catchment contributions to flood behaviour in the target region of the Kalu Ganga River basin. The study demonstrated the value of interpretable machine learning approaches for strengthening flood forecasting and disaster preparedness in Sri Lanka.

The study resulted in the following publication:

### 2.1.4 Improving the Accuracy of Automobile RPM Sensing Using Ignition Pulses <span class="status-badge status-completed">Completed</span> {#rpm-sensing}

This study investigated signal processing techniques to improve the accuracy of automobile RPM sensing using ignition pulses from distributor-based ignition systems. By utilizing the primary ignition pulse signal, the research proposed a less mechanically intrusive alternative to conventional sensing methods and evaluated multiple filter designs to improve measurement reliability. The findings showed that a simple RC filter could effectively mitigate noise and transients while enabling accurate and stable RPM detection, highlighting the potential of the method for embedded automotive systems and vehicle retrofitting applications.

Publication arising from this study:

### 2.1.7 Encrypted Radio for Defence Forces <span class="status-badge status-completed">Completed</span> {#encrypted-radio}

This undergraduate research project focused on the design and development of a secure analogue radio communication platform for defence applications in Sri Lanka. Conducted in collaboration with the **Centre for Defence Research and Development, Ministry of Defence, Sri Lanka**, the study addressed the need for a low-cost, field-operable, and secure tactical communication system suitable for local operational requirements. The project introduced an analogue FM radio system operating in VHF and UHF bands and incorporated multiple security-oriented mechanisms, including audio encryption and frequency hopping spread spectrum, to strengthen confidentiality, integrity, and availability in tactical communication. The study further investigated transceiver selection, embedded interface design, synchronisation mechanisms, and hardware implementation, resulting in a functional prototype with strong potential for further development toward a deployable defence communication platform.

### 2.2.2 Animal Repellent System Design, Deployment, and Impact Analysis <span class="status-badge status-ongoing">Ongoing</span> {#animal-repellent-system}

More than **400 repellent devices** have since been deployed across Sri Lanka, and the technology has been recognized by relevant government organizations as a sustainable solution for animal intrusion management. A field survey representing **75 users** and approximately 100 deployed devices recorded a **98% satisfaction rate**, indicating strong user acceptance and practical effectiveness in real-world agricultural contexts.
